The ontological shift

From social media feeds to social worlds

Web 2.0Posts, likes, photos, location.
Virtual worldsGaze, motion, embodiment, proximity.
Ethical shiftSocial interaction becomes measurable and visceral.
  • The metaverse moves networking from feeds into persistent, real-time, avatar-mediated spaces.
  • Users socialize through bodies, proximity, voice, movement, and shared environments.
  • These are not old internet problems with 3D graphics; the body is now part of the interface.
When virtual harm is felt by the body, "virtual" stops being a defense.

Framework

Five ethical domains in online virtual worlds

01

Biometric privacy

Headsets collect eye, motion, and spatial signals that can identify or profile users.
02

Psychological impact

Avatar embodiment can change behavior and intensify harassment or consent violations.
03

Social interaction harms

Spatial harassment, proximity abuse, and weak consent controls create psychological safety risks.
04

Digital autonomy

Avatar theft attacks self-expression, identity, and safe participation in social spaces.
05

Economic exploitation

Creator economies and virtual currencies can bypass labor and financial safeguards.

Biometric surveillance

Your body becomes the login

  • Eye tracking supports foveated rendering, but it also records involuntary attention patterns.
  • Pupil dilation, fixation, and saccades can imply cognitive load, emotion, and health signals.
  • A biometric pattern cannot be changed like a password after exposure.
CaptureEyes, motion, space.
InferAttention, mood, intent.
IdentifyRe-link anonymous users.
MonetizeTarget and profile.
ID

Eye-tracking research cited in the draft shows ordinary VR tasks can support unique re-identification.

Psychological phenomena

The mind follows the avatar

01

The Proteus Effect

Avatar traits can subconsciously shift behavior, confidence, negotiation style, and bias expression.
02

Phantom Touch Illusion

Users can report tingling, warmth, pressure, or pain when an avatar is touched.
03

VR Dissociation

Prolonged immersion can trigger depersonalization or derealization, changing risk assessment after headset removal.
Presented by: Member 2
Social interaction harms

Harassment becomes spatial

  • In virtual worlds, harassment is not only text or voice. It can be proximity, swarming, cornering, and aggressive gestures.
  • The updated draft cites the Horizon Worlds Exposed report, where researchers observed abuse during 100 visits to popular social spaces.
  • Safety controls can fail when they mute speech but do not remove the harasser's visible avatar from shared space.
100

Visits analyzed in the cited Horizon Worlds report, documenting racist, misogynistic, homophobic, and minor-directed abuse.

Presented by: Member 2
Digital identity and autonomy

Avatar theft is social identity theft

  • In VRChat, custom avatars can represent hundreds of hours or real money.
  • Ripping uses modded clients or cache extraction to copy Unity avatar files.
  • Creators self-censor and avoid public worlds when their digital body can be cloned.
01Modified client extracts avatar data.
02Blueprint ID is bypassed or forged.
03Avatar is sold, worn, or bundled with malware.

Case Study 03

Roblox and the child labor economy

  • The updated draft cites Roblox as having 70M+ daily users and relying on underage creator labor.
  • In 2023, the platform reportedly flagged more than 13,000 child exploitation incidents.
  • Predators can use open social features to start contact, then move conversations to encrypted apps like Discord.
  • Financial investigations cited in the draft suggest trust and safety spending fell 2% year over year.

Case Study 04

VRChat and the black market of digital identity

  • A custom avatar is a body, a reputation, and a social signal.
  • Ripper forums convert unique digital bodies into files for resale or impersonation.
  • Users retreat from public worlds when identity protection is technically weak.
  • Some rely on third-party mesh scramblers because platform-level protections remain insufficient.
Body

The ethical harm is not only copyright theft. It is the theft of a chosen social presence.

Presented by: Member 4
Countermeasures

Safety has to be designed into the world

01

Privacy tech

Apply k-anonymity, differential privacy, and Fourier perturbation before biometric data leaves the headset.
02

Real-time moderation

Use federated AI to detect toxic behavior and trigger safety bubbles within milliseconds.
03

Zero Trust

Continuously authenticate avatars, devices, APIs, biometric transfers, and in-world transactions.
04

Responsible innovation

Use anticipatory governance, public debate, and human-centered boundaries before deployment.
Presented by: Member 4
Conclusion

Reform across three dimensions

01

Jurisprudence

Recognize virtual crimes as real crimes, including theft of property, identity, and psychological safety.

02

Platform governance

End absolute TOS shields and require a duty of care for platforms that monetize minors and biometric data.

03

Ethics-by-design

Integrate bioethicists, psychologists, labor advocates, legal scholars, and end-users before harm becomes infrastructure.

The future of online virtual worlds is a policy choice, not a technical inevitability.
